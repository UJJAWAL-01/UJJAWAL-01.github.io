Fibonacci Central Pivot Range

A range-based pivot framework that fuses Central Pivot Range (CPR) geometry with Fibonacci scaling to produce sharper, structurally-aware support/resistance zones and higher-probability entry/exit bands.

Fibonacci Central Pivot Range (F‑CPR)

Overview: Traditional pivot systems compute single levels which can misrepresent price structure during volatile regimes. F‑CPR defines a central pivot range (CPR) and refines support/resistance using Fibonacci ratios (e.g., 0.382, 0.5, 0.618) applied to derived S/R spreads. This produces adaptive bands that align with both volume-weighted structure (VWAP) and short-term volatility (ATR), improving the precision of entries, stop placements and profit targets.

Algorithm (high-level)

  2. Daily pivot geometry (using previous-day OHLC):
    • Central pivot range (CPR) is computed from prior session OHLC and used to derive intra-day bands.

  4. Fibonacci refinement: For each base S/R pair, compute Fibonacci-scaled offsets and overlay them as intermediate bands: 
    S1_f_r = S1 + r × (P - S1)
R1_f_r = R1 - r × (R1 - P)
    where r ∈ {0.382, 0.5, 0.618}.
  5. Technical overlays:
    • VWAP — volume-weighted typical price to bias bias and intraday fair-value.
    • ATR — Average True Range for volatility scaling.
    • SuperTrend bands — HL2 ± (Factor × ATR), used for trend confirmation and trailing stop placement.
  6. Decision rules (examples):

    • Short bias when price < VWAP and below SuperTrend upper band; prefer entries at Fibonacci-refined R1_f_0.618 with ATR-scaled stop above R2.
Why Fibonacci adds value

Applying Fibonacci ratios to the CPR-derived spreads converts single-point pivots into probabilistic bands that frequently coincide with natural retracement levels and liquidity clusters. Empirically, this reduces noise in entry signals and yields tighter risk-adjusted returns vs. classical single-level pivot rules.

Drifted Bollinger Bands

A multi-filtered Bollinger breakout strategy using long-term trend (365 EMA), short-term volatility gating, fixed risk rules and dynamic trailing stops to capture directional drifts while filtering false breakouts.

Drifted Bollinger Bands (DBB)

Overview: DBB is a structurally disciplined breakout system. It signals entries on Bollinger Band breakouts but only when the long-term trend and a volatility filter agree. The approach reduces whipsaws and focuses capital on high-probability drift moves. Risk is controlled via fixed stop-loss & take-profit, with an optional ATR-based trailing stop to lock gains during strong trends.

Formal definitions (high level)

The strategy uses standard Bollinger components (center SMA, band width via standard deviation) combined with trend and volatility screening. Exact algebraic expressions and custom adjustments are available on request.

Algorithm (DBB v1)
  1. Inputs: BB_length = 14, BB_k = 2.1, TrendEMA = 365, Vol_SD_len = 15, Vol_SMA_len = 28, StopLoss (fixed %), TakeProfit (fixed %), ATR_len = 14 (for trailing), Trail_ATR_mult.
  2. Compute Bollinger Bands using BB_length and BB_k.
  3. Trend filter: compute EMA_{365} on Close. Require: For Long trades price > EMA_{365}; for Short trades price < EMA_{365}.
  4. Volatility filter: compute SD_{15} (current volatility) and its 28-period SMA. Only allow trades when SD_{15} > SMA_{28}(SD_{15}). This ensures volatility is above recent average.
  5. Generate entry signals:
    • Long entry: when Close crosses above Upper Band AND Trend filter = Long AND Volatility filter = true.
    • Short entry: when Close crosses below Lower Band AND Trend filter = Short AND Volatility filter = true.
  6. Risk rules (fixed): place StopLoss at a fixed distance (e.g. 1.5% or ATR_scaled) from entry. Set initial TakeProfit at a multiple (e.g. 1.5×) of risk (R). Use position sizing to cap risk per trade (e.g. 0.5–1.0% of equity).
  7. Trailing stop (optional): after trade moves favorably by X pips or Y × ATR, convert stop to ATR_len × Trail_ATR_mult below (for long) / above (for short) price and move it up as price advances to secure gains.

Risk management & performance notes

DBB intentionally trades only when volatility and trend align with a breakout to avoid structural noise. Typical performance characteristics observed in backtests:

  • Lower trade frequency vs naive BB breakouts, higher win-rate due to filtering.
  • Improved maximum drawdown vs unfiltered breakouts when combined with fixed stop sizing and ATR-based trailing stops.
  • Works best on liquid, intraday tickers where the 365 EMA provides meaningful structural bias (e.g., futures, FX majors).
Implementation notes

Ensure all indicators use the same bar timeframe. When using percent fixed stops, convert percent to instrument ticks for precision. For portfolio deployment, calibrate trade frequency and volatility thresholds per instrument to avoid overfitting.

Gap Reversal Capture

Targets reversal opportunities after significant market-open gaps using gap magnitude, RSI exhaustion and ATR-based adaptive stops to capture directional mean-reversions.

GAPR — Gap Reversal Capture

Overview: GAPR identifies high-probability reversal setups at the open by combining a gap threshold with short-term momentum exhaustion (RSI) and volatility-scaled risk management. The system targets limit reversion while adapting stop/target levels to current instrument volatility via ATR.

Key inputs
  • Gap threshold (percentage) — defines what constitutes a significant open gap.
  • RSI length & thresholds — overbought/oversold levels to confirm exhaustion.
  • ATR length & multipliers — for dynamic stop-loss and target sizing.
Rules (high level)
  1. Compute gap % = (Today’s Open − Yesterday’s Close) / Yesterday’s Close × 100.
  2. Identify Gap Up (≥ threshold) and Gap Down (≤ −threshold).
  3. Calculate RSI over the chosen lookback window.
  4. Entry signals:
    • Short when Gap Up AND RSI indicates overbought (e.g., >70).
    • Long when Gap Down AND RSI indicates oversold (e.g., <30).
  5. Risk management: use ATR-derived stop and target distances; scale position to fixed % risk per trade.
